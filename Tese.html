Using embeddings to infer diagnostics from medical prescriptions

Medical records are an important tool to assess the health status of populations. One example of such data are medical prescriptions, which have increasingly migrated from paper to digital records. The main goals of prescriptions are: providing patients with instructions about their care, controlling the sale of substances with potential side-effects when used off-label, and keeping track of expenses. At the same time, they are a rich dataset to glimpse into the health status of populations, as well as the prescribing habits of medical doctors. However, they are often not associated with information on diagnostics.

In this project, we want to harness the ability of neural embeddings to identify relationships between elements to estimate the prevalence of chronic disease. We will make use of the database of electronic prescriptions in Portugal, which covers over 95% of all prescriptions since 2017. It includes 244 million prescriptions to 12 million distinct patients between January 2017 and December 2021. With these data, we will train embedding models using the list of drugs prescribed to each patient. We will then identify groups of co-prescribed drugs and evaluate whether or not they correspond to specific diseases.

As a ground-truth dataset, we will use lists of drugs that have been manually curated by an expert and which correspond five medical conditions - diabetes, asthma + COPD, coronary heart disease, chronic liver disease and chronic neurological disease. This will require identifying the best hyperparameters and designing and improving a clustering algorithm able to capture the variability in the data (namely different number of drugs per disease and different distances between them).

Once the algorithm is fine tuned, we will be able to analyse the prevalence of different diseases in the Portuguese population and the role of the Covid-19 pandemic in the number of new diagnoses.




The Master's thesis will be developed in the 2nd semester of 2023/2024. The project will be co-supervised by LIP researchers Lília Perfeito and Joana Gonçalves de Sá, both from the SPAC Lab (https://socialcomplexity.eu) at LIP, and by Bruno Martins (http://web.ist.utl.pt/bruno.g.martins/) from IST and INESC-ID.
